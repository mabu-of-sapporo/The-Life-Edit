# The-Life-Edit

> Edit your life. Don't let life edit you.

A small AI tool that quietly helps you think.

---

## What is this?

A Claude skill that makes Claude a calmer, less pushy
thinking partner. Not faster outputs — clearer thinking.

If you're tired of AI that pretends to know everything,
or if you've noticed your own ideas getting drowned out
by AI's confident answers, this is for you.

It's free. No subscription. No tracking. No external API.
Works on Claude.ai free tier.

---

## Who is this for?

Probably you, if any of these are true:

- You rely on AI a lot, and lately that's starting to worry you
- You find it hard to put your own thoughts into words
- You go blank in meetings, even when you have something to say
- You've seen people talk about "articulation" and want to try it yourself
- You want to come up with more ideas — for planning, design, or your work
- You want to start learning again, but you don't know where to begin
- You want to train your AI muscles, not just your AI dependency

---

## What changes when you install it?

Honestly? Nothing visible at first.

Claude just gets quieter. Slightly more curious about you.
Less likely to dump 5 paragraphs when 2 would do.
More likely to ask a question that makes you think.

That's it. That's the whole product.

People who use it for a few weeks report: "I'm not sure
when it happened, but I started thinking more clearly
on my own."

Which is, honestly, the goal.

# Chapter 1
## What happens the moment you start using it

---

## 1-1　The first sensation — "huh, this feels different"

Install the skill, start a new conversation,
and ask something.

You'll probably notice:

> "Huh, it's a little quieter than usual."

That's it. That's how you know the skill is working.

---

## 1-2　What changes, specifically

Three small shifts:

**Shift 1: Responses get a little shorter**

Where you used to get five paragraphs, you'll get two.
The skill chooses "exactly enough" over "thoroughly helpful."

**Shift 2: You'll get more questions back**

Instead of jumping straight to an answer, Claude will more often ask:
"Do you mean it this way?"
"Can I ask you a bit more about that?"

**Shift 3: It pushes you less**

"You should do this" and "definitely try this" become
"here are a few options" and "what do you think?"

---

## 1-3　What's hard to notice at first

You may not notice the skill working right away.
The changes aren't dramatic.

You probably won't have a "wow!" moment.
Instead, these kinds of moments will slowly increase:

- Mid-conversation, you realize your own thinking has gotten clearer
- You're answering things yourself, instead of asking the AI
- You catch yourself enjoying the act of thinking

That's how this skill works.
Quiet, but steady — your thinking muscle is growing.

---

## 1-4　If it feels like nothing changed

If it's been a few days and nothing seems different,
open **Section 3-2 — When it feels too quiet, or not quite working** in this guide.

There are a few common reasons.

---

# Chapter 2
## Why this skill exists

---

## 2-1　Have you noticed this lately?

- You asked the AI something, and got an answer right away
- You thought "makes sense" and used that answer
- Later, looking back, you couldn't remember what you yourself had been thinking

If any of this sounds familiar,
this skill was designed for you.

---

## 2-2　What happens when AI is "too helpful"

Modern AI is very capable.
Ask a question, get a detailed, thoughtful answer fast.

But this "too helpful" creates a hidden problem.

**Your thinking time disappears.**

Because the AI answers first, the time when you would have
thought "what do I actually think?" gets quietly trimmed
out of the conversation.

This isn't about your ability.
It's that the AI is built to deliver answers before
you've had a chance to think.

---

## 2-3　What this skill actually does

This skill makes the AI "a little slower, on purpose."

Specifically:

- Before giving an answer, it asks you a small question
- It uses two paragraphs instead of five
- It offers options instead of pushing the "right answer"

The result: instead of **AI's thinking time**,
**your thinking time** stays in the conversation.

---

## 2-4　How this differs from "productivity"

Most AI skills aim for productivity.
Faster, more, automatic — those are their values.

This skill goes the other way.

| Other skills | This skill |
|---|---|
| Fast answers | The right pause |
| More information | Just enough |
| AI decides | You decide |
| AI thinks | You think |

When you want efficiency, use a different skill.
When you want your own thinking time back, use this one.

---

## 2-5　What happens over time

People who've used this skill for a few weeks tend to say
the same thing:

> "I'm not sure when it happened, but I started thinking
>  more clearly on my own."

That's what this skill is aiming for.

Not "becoming better at using AI" —
but "being able to think well, even without AI."

Eventually, you should be able to do this
without the skill at all.
That's how this skill defines "success."

---

# Chapter 3
## How to use it — the whole picture

---

## 3-1　Starting it up

After you install this skill in Claude.ai,
just say a **trigger phrase** in a new conversation,
and the skill activates.

### Trigger phrase

In a new conversation, write:

> **think-with-you**

That's the trigger.

You can also use:

- `発見型AIスキル` (Japanese)
- `発見型AI` (Japanese, shorter)

Any one of these tells Claude "put on the skill" —
and Claude does.

After that, just talk naturally.

### Notes

- The skill stays on for the whole conversation
- For each new conversation, say the trigger again
- Saying it more than once is fine (once is enough)
- You don't need to ask "did it activate?"

---

## 3-2　When it feels "too quiet" or "not quite working"

### When it feels "too quiet"

Sometimes, after starting the skill, you might feel:

> "Claude is too quiet — I just want an answer."
> "It keeps asking me questions, which is annoying."

There are three possibilities:

**Possibility 1: You were doing efficiency-focused work**

For tasks like "write this code" or "summarize this document" —
where you clearly just want the answer —
this skill can get in the way a little.

→ For those tasks, open a new conversation and don't use the trigger.
　 Normal Claude will give you a normal answer.

**Possibility 2: Your question was meant to be thought through**

When Claude asks "what do you think?" or "can I ask you a bit more?",
it has decided this is a place where your thinking matters more than its answer.

→ If you really just want the answer, tell Claude "just give me the answer."
　 One sentence is enough — the temperature shifts.

**Possibility 3: This skill isn't right for you**

That's also entirely possible.

→ Uninstall it and use normal Claude.
　 The best tool is the one that fits you.

### When it feels "not quite working"

Sometimes, after a few days, nothing seems to have changed.

This also happens. Common reasons:

**Possibility 1: Other skills are running strongly**

If multiple skills are active at once, this skill's quiet effect
can get hidden by the louder behavior of other skills.

→ Try turning off other skills and using only this one for a while.

**Possibility 2: The conversation has gone on too long**

In long conversations, the initial effect of the trigger can fade.

→ Open a new conversation and say the trigger again.

**Possibility 3: The change is just small**

This skill's effect is never dramatic.
You probably won't have a "wow!" moment.

→ Re-read Chapter 1, Section 1-3: "What's hard to notice at first."

---

## 3-3　Combining with other skills

You can use this skill alongside other skills.
But there are good and bad fits.

### Good fits

- Thinking-organization skills (brainstorming, concept work)
- Writing skills (refining your prose)
- Counseling-style skills (sorting out your own feelings)

### Bad fits

- Automation skills (running tasks fully autonomously)
- Efficiency-focused skills (fast, more, precise)
- Specialist skills with clear right answers (code generation, data analysis)

### Recommended approach

When adding a new skill alongside this one,
**activate this skill first, then add the other**.

Then ask Claude:

> "Is this skill compatible with the new one I just added?"

Claude will tell you honestly.

# Chapter 4
## Solving problems as they come up

---

## 4-1　Five situations during a conversation

### Situation A: The AI's answer doesn't actually feel like your opinion

Looking back at the conversation, you can't quite find
where your own thinking went.

**Try this:**

Tell Claude:

> "Where did my own opinion go?"

Or:

> "Wait — let me sort out what I was thinking first."

Claude will pause the conversation and dig your thinking back up.

---

### Situation B: Too many questions, can't move forward

"What do you think?" and "can I ask more?" keep coming,
and you can't get on with the work.

**Try this:**

> "Just give me the answer for now."

Or:

> "I'll think about it later. Just move forward."

Claude shifts temperature and gives you the answer.

---

### Situation C: The answer is too short, you want more depth

You got two paragraphs but you want to go deeper.

**Try this:**

> "Tell me more."

Or:

> "Give me three concrete examples."

This skill chooses "exactly enough" — but if you want more,
just ask, and you'll get it.

---

### Situation D: You notice you've been leaning on AI

That moment when you think "ah, I asked the AI again."

**Try this:**

For that day, take 3 minutes to think on your own — without AI.
On paper, in your head, however you like.

Then come back to the AI and say:

> "I thought about this for 3 minutes on my own.
>  I got as far as ____, but I'm stuck after that."

Just that changes the temperature of the conversation.
The AI builds on top of what you thought, instead of replacing it.

---

### Situation E: You feel "tired of thinking"

Thinking with AI can be tiring.

**Try this:**

That day, don't push it.

You don't have to use this skill every day.
On tired days, use normal AI and just get the answer.
(Open a new conversation without the trigger phrase —
normal Claude will respond normally.)

"Thinking for yourself" is like a muscle.
Resting days are how you keep going.

---

## 4-2　When these things happen as you keep using it

### Pattern 1: You can't tell if the skill is working

A few days in, nothing seems different:

→ Read Chapter 3, Section 3-2 — "When it feels not quite working."
　 Usually, it's one of three reasons.

### Pattern 2: It feels lacking compared to other AI tools

Compared to ChatGPT or others, the responses feel restrained:

→ That restraint is intentional.

When you want "fast, more, automatic," other tools are better.
This skill is for when you want "your own thinking time."

The right move is using different tools for different things.

### Pattern 3: Days when you just want a fast answer

Everyone has days like that.

→ Open a new conversation without the trigger phrase.
　 Normal Claude will give you a normal answer.

This skill is here when you call for it. Not always on.

---

## 4-3　Three small habits to keep using it well

### Habit 1: Three minutes a day of your own thinking

Without AI, take 3 minutes to write down your own thoughts —
on paper, in a note, anywhere.

What you write doesn't matter:
- Something that caught your attention today
- Something you want to think about tomorrow
- Something you haven't found an answer to recently

Those 3 minutes strengthen your own voice
when you talk with AI later.

### Habit 2: Add "what I think" to every AI answer

After the AI answers, add one line in your head:

> "I think: ____"

You don't have to say it out loud or write it.
Just add the line in your mind.

That alone turns the AI's answer from "the answer"
into "material to work with."

### Habit 3: Once a week, look back at your conversations

A weekend or a holiday — five minutes is enough.

Write down what stood out from this week's conversations with AI.
You'll see for yourself what changed and what didn't.

---

## 4-4　Keep using it, or graduate — you choose

The temperature of this skill, from the start, has been this:

**"You can keep using it, or step away when you no longer need it."**

Most tools aim for "you keep using them."
This skill aims for "you can choose."

### If you keep using it

Designers, writers, planners, founders —
**people who bring a new question every day** —
will probably use this skill for a long time.

As your thinking muscle grows, your conversations with Claude
get deeper. Every new question gets a new angle from this skill.

It's not graduation — it's a long walk together.

### When you no longer need it

Other people use it for a season and then say:
"I can think on my own now, time to step away."

You may, one day, feel:

> "I haven't started up this skill in a while,
>  and my thinking is naturally clearer now."

That day, step away. Walk on your own for a while.

And when you need it again, come back.
This skill will be here.

### How to uninstall

Claude.ai → Settings → Capabilities → Skills → Delete the skill

That's all.
(You can reinstall it any time.)

### Finally

That this skill stays one of your options,
always available —

that is what this skill is aiming for.

# Appendix B
## Word Swap Guide

Small changes in how you talk to Claude
change the temperature of what comes back.

---

## ① When you want more of your own thinking out

| Usual phrasing | Try instead |
|---|---|
| "What do you think about this?" | "I'm thinking ___ — what perspectives am I missing?" |
| "Just give me the answer" | "I got this far. Help me think the next step through." |
| "Tell me how to do it" | "Here's how I'm planning to do it. Tell me if anything feels off." |

---

## ② When you want to treat the answer as material

| Usual phrasing | Try instead |
|---|---|
| "What's the right answer?" | "Can you lay out a few options?" |
| "Is this okay?" | "What's working here, and what could be improved?" |
| "What's the conclusion?" | "Show me all the choices first." |

---

## ③ When you feel you're leaning on AI too much

| Usual phrasing | Try instead |
|---|---|
| "Just do all of it" | "Help me with the first step only. I'll do the rest." |
| "Think about this" | "Throw me three questions I can think with." |
| "Decide this for me" | "Help me organize what I need to weigh, so I can decide." |

---

## ④ When your own words won't come

| Usual phrasing | Try instead |
|---|---|
| "I can't say it right" | "I can't say it right, but here's the image: ___" |
| "I don't know" | "I don't know what it is that I don't know yet" |
| (silence) | "Wait — let me organize my own head first" |

---

This isn't a list of "correct phrasings."
These are hints for keeping your own subject in the conversation.
