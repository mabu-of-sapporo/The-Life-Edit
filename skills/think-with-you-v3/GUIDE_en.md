# The-Life-Edit

> Edit your life.
> So that life does not edit you.

A small tool — not for letting AI "do the thinking,"
but for making it easier for **you** to think.

---

## What is this?

A free Claude skill.
It makes Claude a quieter, slightly less pushy thinking partner.

If you've ever felt the unease of —
"I just nodded along to the AI's answer,
 and somewhere along the way, my own thinking time disappeared" —
this skill is for you.

It's free. No subscription. Works on Claude's free tier.

---

## Who is this designed for?

Probably people like:

- Someone who relies on AI too much, and that's starting to feel uneasy
- Someone who finds it hard to express themselves in their own words
- Someone who goes blank in meetings or discussions
- Someone curious about the recent trend of "putting things into language"
- Someone who wants to generate many ideas in planning or design
- Someone who wants to learn again, but doesn't know where to start
- Someone who wants to train their "AI muscles"

---

## What changes when you install it?

Honestly, at first, nothing visible changes.

Claude becomes a little quieter.
It listens to you a little more carefully.
It writes 2 paragraphs where it used to write 5.
It returns questions a bit more often, instead of answers.

That's it. That's the whole product.

People who've used it for a few weeks say:
"I don't know when it happened, but my own thinking time
 has naturally increased."

That is what this skill is aiming for.

# Chapter 1
## What happens the moment you start using it

---

## 1-1　The first sensation — "Wait, this feels different"

After installing the skill, open a new conversation
and ask Claude something.

You'll probably feel:

> "Hmm, this is a little quieter than usual."

That's it. That's the sign that the skill is working.

---

## 1-2　Specifically, what changes

Three small changes:

**Change 1: Replies get a little shorter**

What used to be answered in 5 paragraphs becomes 2.
Instead of "kindly detailed," it prioritizes "neither more nor less."

**Change 2: Questions increase a little**

Instead of immediately giving answers, Claude returns:
"Did you mean it like this?"
"Can I ask a bit more?"
— more often.

**Change 3: Pushing decreases a little**

"You should do this," "You absolutely must do this" become
"Here are some options," "What do you think?"

---

## 1-3　Things hard to notice at first

It can be hard to notice the skill is working at first,
because the changes aren't dramatic.

A "Wow!" moment probably won't happen.
Instead, these moments will gradually increase:

- Mid-conversation, your own thoughts feel a little organized
- You're coming up with answers yourself, without leaning on AI
- You catch yourself enjoying the act of thinking

That's how this skill works.
Not dramatic, but reliably, your thinking strength is growing.

---

## 1-4　If you feel "nothing has changed"

If, after a few days, nothing feels different,
open **3-2 "When it feels too strong / not working"** in this guide.

There are a few common causes.

---

# Chapter 2
## Why this skill is needed

---

## 2-1　Has this happened to you recently?

- You asked AI a question and got an answer right away
- You thought "ah, makes sense" and used that answer as is
- Looking back later, you can't recall what you yourself were thinking

If you've had this experience,
this skill is designed for you.

---

## 2-2　When AI is "too kind," what happens

Modern AI is very capable.
Ask a question and you get a fast, detailed, polite answer.

But this "too kind" creates a problem.

**Your thinking time disappears.**

Because AI delivers the answer first,
your time to think "what do I think?"
is gradually shaved out of the conversation.

This isn't a problem with your ability.
It's because AI is designed to hand over answers
before you've had a chance to think.

---

## 2-3　What this skill is doing

This skill is a tool for "slowing AI down, just a little."

Specifically:

- Before AI gives an answer, it returns a small question to you
- Where it would write 5 paragraphs, it finishes in 2
- Instead of pushing "the right answer," it lays out "options"

By doing this, **your thinking time** — not AI's thinking time —
remains in the conversation.

---

## 2-4　How this differs from "efficiency"

Most AI skills aim for "efficiency."
Fast, plenty, automatic — that's the value.

This skill is built in the opposite direction.

| Typical skill | This skill |
|---|---|
| Fast answer | Appropriate pause |
| Lots of information | Just enough information |
| AI decides | You decide |
| AI thinks | You think |

When you want efficiency, use a different skill.
When you want time to think for yourself, use this one.

---

## 2-5　What happens if you keep using it

People who've used this skill for several weeks all say something in common:

> "I don't know when, but my own thinking time
>  has naturally increased."

That's what this skill is aiming for.

Not "becoming able to use AI,"
but "becoming able to think for myself, even without AI."

In the end, you don't need this skill —
you yourself can hold the strength to think.
That is this skill's definition of "success."

---

# Chapter 3
## The whole picture of how to use it

---

## 3-1　How to activate

Once installed in Claude.ai, this skill activates simply
by saying a **trigger phrase** in a new conversation.

### Trigger phrases

In a new conversation, write:

> **think-with-you**

That's the activation phrase.

These also activate it:

**English:**

- `think-with-you`

**Japanese (works just as well in English chats):**

- `発見型AIスキル` / `発見型AI` / `発見型` / `AIスキル`
- `立方体` / `立方体スキル` / `ジャイロスコープ`
- `主語ラリー` / `主語を取り戻す`
- `think-with-you-v3`
- `v3 解放` / `発見型AI解放`

Any of these will be received by Claude as
"the signal to put on the garment,"
and the skill will be worn.

After that, just talk naturally.

### Notes

- The skill stays active for the duration of that conversation
- When you start a new conversation, say the trigger phrase again
- You can say it as many times as you like (once is enough)
- No need to ask "did it activate?"

---

## 3-2　When it feels "too strong" or "not working"

### When it feels "too strong"

After starting to use the skill, you may feel:

> "Claude is too quiet — I want a bit more answer"
> "Just being asked questions all the time is annoying"

Three possibilities:

**Possibility 1: You were doing efficiency-focused work**

In situations where you clearly want an answer
("write code," "summarize this text"),
this skill can get a little in the way.

→ For those, open a new conversation without the trigger phrase.
　 Regular Claude will answer normally.

**Possibility 2: The question itself was the thing to think about**

When Claude returns "what do you think?" or "can I ask more?",
it's judging that this is a place where *you* should think.

→ If that bothers you, just tell Claude "I just want the answer."
　 One sentence changes the temperature.

**Possibility 3: This skill didn't suit you**

That happens too.

→ Uninstall and use regular Claude.
　 The right tool for you is the one that fits.

### When it feels "not working"

After a few days, you may feel nothing has changed.

This is also common:

**Possibility 1: Other skills are running strongly**

When using multiple skills at once, this skill's quiet effect
can be hidden by louder ones.

→ Try turning off other skills and using this one alone for a conversation.

**Possibility 2: The conversation has gone on too long**

In long conversations, the initial activation effect can fade.

→ Open a new conversation and say the trigger phrase again.

**Possibility 3: The change is just small**

The changes from this skill aren't dramatic to begin with.
A "Wow!" moment probably won't happen.

→ Read 1-3 "Things hard to notice at first" again.

---

## 3-3　Using with other skills

You can use this skill alongside others.
But there are compatibility issues.

### Good fits

- Thought-organizing skills (brainstorming, concept generation)
- Writing skills (polishing text)
- Counseling-type skills (sorting your emotions)

### Poor fits

- Automation skills (running tasks fully autonomously)
- Efficiency-focused skills (fast / many / accurate)
- Specialist skills (code generation, data analysis — clear-answer work)

### Recommended approach

When combining with another skill,
**activate this skill first, then add the new one**.

Then ask Claude:

> "Are this skill and the new one OK to use together?"

Claude will tell you honestly.

# Chapter 4
## Solutions for difficult situations

---

## 4-1　When stuck mid-conversation — 5 scenes

### Scene A: The AI's answer doesn't feel like your own opinion anymore

Looking back at the conversation, you can't tell where your own thoughts went.

**Try this:**

Tell Claude:

> "Where did my opinion go?"

Or:

> "Wait — let me sort out what I was thinking first."

Claude will pause the conversation and dig back into your thoughts.

---

### Scene B: Too many questions, not making progress

"What do you think?" "Can I ask more?" keeps coming,
and the work isn't moving forward.

**Try this:**

> "I just want the answer for now."

Or:

> "We can think later. I just want to move to the next step."

Claude will switch temperature and give you an answer.

---

### Scene C: Answer is too short — you want more detail

It finished in 2 paragraphs, but you want to go deeper.

**Try this:**

> "Tell me a bit more in detail."

Or:

> "Give me 3 specific examples."

This skill chooses "neither more nor less,"
so when you want detail, just ask and it will respond.

---

### Scene D: You notice you're leaning on AI

"Ah, I asked the AI again" — when you catch yourself doing this.

**Try this:**

For that day, take 3 minutes — without AI — to think on your own.
Write on paper, or just in your head.

After 3 minutes, return to the AI and say:

> "I thought on my own for 3 minutes.
>  I got as far as ○○, but from here I'm not sure."

This alone changes the temperature of the conversation.
The AI will add to "what *you* thought," not start from scratch.

---

### Scene E: You feel "thinking is exhausting"

Thinking together with AI can tire your head.

**Try this:**

That day, don't push.

You don't have to use this skill every day.
On tired days, use regular AI and just take the answer.
(Open a new conversation without the trigger phrase, and regular Claude will run.)

"Thinking for yourself" is like a muscle.
Rest days are what let you keep going.

---

## 4-2　When this happens while using it

### Pattern 1: When you can't tell if the skill is working

When you've used it for a few days and nothing seems different:

→ Read 3-2 "When it feels not working."
　 Most often, the cause is one of the three listed there.

### Pattern 2: When it feels lacking compared to other AI tools

When the response feels muted compared to ChatGPT or others:

→ That is the temperature this skill creates intentionally.

When you want "fast / many / automatic," other tools are better.
This skill shines when you want "time to think for yourself."

Using the right tool for the moment is best.

### Pattern 3: Days when you want "just an answer, fast"

Everyone has those days.

→ Open a new conversation without the trigger phrase.
　 Regular Claude will answer normally.

This skill is enough to call when you need it.

---

## 4-3　Tips for keeping at it — 3 small habits

### Habit 1: Spend 3 minutes a day writing your own thoughts

Without AI, take 3 minutes to write your thoughts on paper or notes.

Write whatever:
- Things you noticed today
- Things you want to think about tomorrow
- Things you haven't found answers to recently

These 3 minutes strengthen "your own voice"
when you talk with AI.

### Habit 2: Add "what I think" — one line — to every AI answer

After AI gives an answer, add this one line in your head:

> "What I think: ____"

You don't have to say it out loud or write it.
Just add one line in your head.

This alone turns AI's answers from "the right answer" into "raw material."

### Habit 3: Once a week, look back at your own conversations

Weekend or holiday — just 5 minutes is enough.

Write down what stood out from your AI conversations this week.
What changed, what didn't change —
you can see it with your own eyes.

---

## 4-4　Keep using, or graduate — you choose

This skill's temperature, from the start, is this:

**"Keep using if you want; leave when you no longer need it."**

Most tools aim for "you keep using us."
This skill aims for "you can choose."

### If you keep using

For designers, writers, planners, leaders —
**people who bring new questions every day** —
you will probably use this skill for a long time.

The more your muscle grows, the deeper your dialogue with Claude becomes.
Each time a new question arrives, this skill hands you a new angle.

It's not "graduation" but "continuing to walk alongside."

### When you no longer need it

On the other hand, some people use it for a period
and then say: "I can think on my own now, so I'll step away for now."

A day might come when you feel:

> "Lately, even without activating this skill,
>  my thoughts come together naturally."

On that day, step away from this skill for a while
and walk with just your own strength.

And when you need it again, come back.
This skill will always be here.

### How to uninstall

Claude.ai → Settings → Capabilities → Skills → Delete this skill

That's all.
(You can reinstall any time you want.)

### Finally

That this skill stays there as one of your options, always —
that is what this skill is aiming for.

# Appendix B
## Paraphrasing table

A small change in how you speak to Claude
changes the temperature of the answer that comes back.

---

## ① When you want to bring out more of your own thinking

| Usual phrasing | Paraphrase |
|---|---|
| "What do you think about this?" | "I'm thinking ○○ — what perspectives am I missing?" |
| "Tell me the answer" | "I've thought this far. Help me think the rest together." |
| "Tell me how to do it" | "I'm going to try it this way. Tell me if anything feels off." |

---

## ② When you want to receive answers as "raw material"

| Usual phrasing | Paraphrase |
|---|---|
| "What's the right answer?" | "Can you lay out a few options?" |
| "Is this OK?" | "Tell me the good points and the things I could improve." |
| "What's the conclusion?" | "Just show me all the choices first." |

---

## ③ When you feel you're depending on AI too much

| Usual phrasing | Paraphrase |
|---|---|
| "Do all of it" | "Help me with just the first step. I'll do the rest." |
| "Think for me" | "Throw me 3 questions for *me* to think with." |
| "Decide for me" | "Organize the material *I* need to make this decision." |

---

## ④ When your own words won't come

| Usual phrasing | Paraphrase |
|---|---|
| "I can't say it well" | "I can't put it well, but the image is something like: ○○" |
| "I don't know" | "I can't yet put into words *what* I don't know." |
| (silence) | "Just give me a moment to sort my head." |

---

This is not a list of "correct phrasings."
It's a hint for ways to keep your own subject in the sentence.
