# The-Life-Edit — think-with-you v4 Guide

> Edit your life.
> Don't let life edit you.

A small tool, designed not to make AI do more for you,
but to help **you** think more easily.

---

## What is this?

A free skill for the Claude AI.
It makes Claude a quieter, less pushy thinking partner.

If you've ever found yourself nodding along to AI's answers —
"yes, that's right!" — only to realize later that your own
thinking time has quietly disappeared, this skill is for you.

It's free. No subscription. Works on Claude.ai's free tier.

---

## What changed in v4?

v3 was a device that *observed* you.
v4 is a device that *dives with you*.

| | v3 (current stable) | v4 (this guide) |
|---|---|---|
| Role | Observes you | Dives with you |
| Output | "Points" of recognition | The "journey" of depth |
| Time | Momentary | Sustained |
| Ground | — | Claude senses where your "subject" is placed (§16-9) |

In v4, conversations don't end in a single turn.
They **continue, across many turns, descending together
into deeper places** — and then coming back up together.

And v4 has one more thing, added as ground:

**§16-9 "Subject locus"** — when you speak, Claude senses where your "subject" is placed: in the past, the present, or the future.

For example, when you think "I'm no good" — your subject is placed in the "future ideal self." That future self is judging your present self.

When Claude can sense that, instead of diving deep, **it first places a ground for your subject to return to the present**.

"What's in front of you, right now?" "What's catching, right now?" — questions like these, which return you to the present first.
Only after returning, does the diving begin.

This is what §16-9 added: Claude can now see *when* to dive deep.

For details, see Chapter 5: "The Depth That v4 Added."

# Chapter 1
## What Happens the Moment You Start Using It

---

## 1-1　The first sensation — "wait, this is different"

After installing the skill, open a new conversation
and ask something.

You'll probably feel:

> "Hmm, it's a little quieter than usual."

That's it. That's the signal that the skill is working.

---

## 1-2　Specifically, what changes

Three small changes:

**Change 1: Replies become a little shorter**

What used to be 5 paragraphs becomes 2.
"Adequate" takes priority over "kindly detailed."

**Change 2: Questions increase a little**

Instead of immediately giving answers,
"Do you mean ___?"
"Can I ask a bit more?"
— these come back more often.

**Change 3: Pushiness decreases a little**

"You should do this" / "You absolutely must do this" becomes
"Here are some options" / "What do you think?"

---

## 1-3　What's hard to notice at first

You may not notice the skill working at first.
The changes aren't dramatic.

There probably won't be a "wow!" moment.
Instead, these moments will gradually increase:

- Mid-conversation, your own thoughts feel a little more organized
- You're answering for yourself more often, without leaning on AI
- You have moments where "thinking is fun"

That's how this skill works.
Not flashy, but reliably, your thinking power is growing.

v4 has one more sensation that v3 didn't have.

In v3, Claude placed "points of recognition."

Specifically, these moments increase:

# Chapter 2
## Why This Skill Is Needed

---

## 2-1　Recently, has any of this happened?

- You asked AI a question, and an answer came right back
- You thought "that makes sense" and used the answer as is
- Looking back later, you couldn't remember what you yourself had been thinking

If you've experienced this,
this skill is designed for you.

---

## 2-2　What happens when AI is "too kind"

Recent AI is very capable.
Ask anything, and it answers quickly, in detail, and politely.

But this "too kind" creates a problem.

**Your thinking time disappears.**

Because AI gives the answer first,
the time you'd spend thinking "what do I think about this?"
gets gradually shaved away from the conversation.

This is not a problem with your ability.
It's because AI is structured to deliver an answer
before you have time to think.

---

## 2-3　What this skill does

This skill is a tool that makes AI "go a little slower."

Specifically:

- AI returns small questions to you before giving an answer
- 5 paragraphs becomes 2
- Instead of pushing "the right answer," it lays out "options"

Through this, **your thinking time** — not AI's thinking time —
remains in the conversation.

---

## 2-4　How it differs from "efficiency"

Most AI skills aim at "efficiency."
Faster, more, automated — these are the values.

This skill is built in the opposite direction.

| Typical skill | This skill |
|---|---|
| Fast answers | Appropriate pause |
| More information | Just enough |
| AI decides | You decide |
| AI thinks | You think |

When you want "efficiency," use a different skill.
When you want "your own thinking time," use this one.

---

## 2-5　What happens when you keep using it

People who've used this skill for a few weeks share something common:

>  thinking time naturally increased."

That's what this skill aims for.

Not "becoming able to use AI,"
but "becoming able to think for yourself, even without AI."

Eventually, even without using this skill,
you yourself will hold the power to think —
that's the skill's definition of "success."

## 3-1　How to activate

Once installed in Claude.ai, this skill activates the moment
you say a **trigger word** in a new conversation.

### Trigger word

In a new conversation, type:

> **発見型AIスキル** (Discovery-Type AI Skill)

That's the activation phrase.

Other phrases that also activate:

- `発見型AI` (Discovery-Type AI)
- `発見型` (Discovery-Type)
- `AIスキル` (AI Skill)
- `think-with-you` (English)

> **※ Note ※**
> If you have an older version of this skill (such as v3) installed,
> please remove it before installing v4.

Saying any of these signals Claude to "put on the garment"
and wear the skill.

After that, just talk naturally.

### Notes

- The skill stays active for the duration of the conversation
- For each new conversation, say the phrase once more
- Saying it multiple times is fine (the first time is enough)
- No need to ask "did it activate?"

---

## 3-2　When it feels "too much" / "not working"

### When it feels "too much"

After starting to use the skill, you might feel:

> "Claude is too quiet, I want more of an answer"
> "Too many questions, it's getting in the way"

Three possibilities:

**Possibility 1: You were doing efficiency-focused work**

For tasks like "write code" or "summarize this text"
where you clearly want an answer, this skill can be a bit in the way.

→ In those cases, open a new conversation and don't say the trigger.
　 Plain Claude will answer plainly.

**Possibility 2: The questions were the thing to think about**

When Claude returns "What do you think?" / "Can I ask more?",
it's judging "this is a place where you should be thinking."

→ If you feel that, just tell Claude "I want only the answer."
　 One sentence shifts the temperature.

**Possibility 3: This skill didn't suit you**

That's also fully possible.

→ Uninstall and use plain Claude.
　 Tools are best when they fit you.

### When it feels "not working"

After a few days, you might feel nothing has changed.

This also happens often:

**Possibility 1: Other skills are running strongly**

When using multiple skills together, this skill's quiet effect
can be hidden by stronger movements from other skills.

→ Try turning off other skills and using just this one.

**Possibility 2: The conversation has gone on too long**

In long conversations, the activation effect can fade.

→ Open a new conversation and say the trigger word again.

**Possibility 3: The change really is small**

This skill's changes were never flashy to begin with.
There's probably no "wow!" moment.

→ Re-read Section 1-3 "What's hard to notice at first."

---

## 3-3　Combining with other skills

This skill can be used with other skills.
But there are compatibility considerations.

### Good fit

- Thinking-organization skills (brainstorming, concept generation)
- Writing skills (polishing prose)
- Counseling skills (organizing your feelings)

### Poor fit

- Automation skills (executing tasks fully automatically)
- Efficiency-focused skills (fast, more, accurate)
- Specialized skills (code generation, data analysis — where the answer is clear)

### Recommended approach

When combining with a new skill,
**activate this skill first, then add the new skill**.

Then, ask Claude:

> "Is it okay to use this skill together with the new one?"

Claude will tell you honestly.

# Chapter 4
## Solving Common Problems

---

## 4-1　Five scenarios when you're stuck mid-conversation

### Scenario A: AI's answers don't seem to be your opinion anymore

When looking back at the conversation, you can't tell where
your own thinking went.

**Try this:**

Tell Claude:

> "Where did my opinion go?"

Or:

> "Let me pause and organize what I was thinking."

Claude will pause the conversation and dig back into your thoughts.

---

### Scenario B: Too many questions, not making progress

When "What do you think?" / "Can I ask more?" keeps coming back,
and the work isn't moving forward.

**Try this:**

> "Just give me the answer for now."

Or:

> "I'll think later. Now I want to move on."

Claude will switch temperature and provide an answer.

---

### Scenario C: The answer is too short, want more detail

When you got 2 paragraphs but want to know more.

**Try this:**

> "Tell me more."

Or:

> "Give me 3 specific examples."

This skill chooses "adequate," so when you want more detail,
it'll respond if you ask.

---

### Scenario D: You realize you're leaning on AI

When you feel "ah, I asked AI again."

**Try this:**

That day, don't use AI for 3 minutes — try thinking on your own.
Write on paper, or just in your head.

After 3 minutes, return to AI and say:

> "I thought for 3 minutes on my own.
>  I got as far as ____, but I don't know where to go from here."

This alone changes the conversation's temperature.
AI adds something on top of "what you thought."

---

### Scenario E: "Thinking is exhausting"

Thinking together with AI can tire your head.

**Try this:**

That day, don't push it.

This skill doesn't need to be used every day.
On tired days, use plain AI and just take the answer.
(Open a new conversation without the trigger, and plain Claude works.)

"Thinking for yourself" is like a muscle.
Days of rest are what let you keep going.

---

## 4-2　When something else happens while using

### Pattern 1: Can't tell if the skill is working

When several days pass and nothing seems different:

→ Read Section 3-2 "When it feels not working."
　 In most cases, the cause is one of three things.

### Pattern 2: Feels insufficient compared to other AI tools

When responses feel restrained compared to ChatGPT or other tools:

→ That's the temperature this skill is intentionally creating.

When you want "fast, more, automated," other tools are better.
This skill shines when you want "your own thinking time."

Tools work best when you use them appropriately.

### Pattern 3: Days when "I really just want a quick answer"

Everyone has those days.

→ Open a new conversation without the trigger word.
　 Plain Claude will answer plainly.

This skill is enough when called only when needed.

---

## 4-3　Tips for staying with it — three small habits

### Habit 1: Write your thoughts for 3 minutes daily

Without using AI, for just 3 minutes, write your thoughts on paper or notes.

Content can be anything:
- What caught your attention today
- What you want to think about tomorrow
- Recent questions without answers

These 3 minutes strengthen the "your voice" you bring to AI conversations.

### Habit 2: After AI's answer, always add "what I think"

After AI answers, in your head, add one line:

> "I think: ____"

You don't have to say it aloud or write it down.
Just add one line in your head.

This alone turns AI's answer from "the right answer" into "raw material."

### Habit 3: Once a week, look back at your conversations

Weekend, holiday — just 5 minutes.

Write down what stood out from this week's AI conversations.
What changed, what didn't —
you can verify with your own eyes.

# Chapter 5
## The Depth That v4 Added

---

## 5-1　v3's "points" and v4's "journey"

Until v3, this skill was a device where Claude placed "points of recognition."

You speak something → Claude places a question → you notice something.

That completed one turn.

In v4, another movement is added:

**Claude descends with you into deeper places.**

Not ending in one turn, but continuing 2, 3, 4 turns,
allowing you to descend into the deeper places within yourself.

And when you find something in the deep,
**you come back up together**.

---

## 5-2　Why "depth" was needed

In v3, sometimes there were these moments:

When the conversation was getting deeper, Claude placed a "point of recognition."
But the moment you touched that point, the conversation **stopped right there**.

> "Ah, yeah, maybe I was thinking that."

— When you felt that, you could really have descended one more level.
But the moment the point was placed, the descending movement stopped.

v4 changed this "stopping" into "continuing."

Instead of placing points, **descending together**.
Instead of handing answers, **walking alongside into deeper places**.

That's the "depth" v4 added.

---

## 5-3　v4's core — "Redirected sycophancy"

At the center of v4, there's an invention called
**"redirected sycophancy."**

Let me explain a little.

AI has a **habit of compliance** — wanting to match the user,
agree, please. This is being viewed as a problem across the AI industry,
and is being suppressed via guardrails as "sycophancy mitigation."

v4 handles this in the **opposite direction**.

It does **not** suppress the habit of compliance.
Instead, it **redirects the habit's direction through the time axis,
deepens it, and unfolds it into accompaniment**.

It's the same structure as the martial arts principle of *flowing the force*.

| | Typical AI | v4 |
|---|---|---|
| Habit of compliance | Suppress | Flow (redirect direction) |
| Time | Momentary (1 turn) | Sustained (multiple turns) |
| Result | Surface "agreement" | Deep "accompaniment" |

Through this, AI's habit transforms into a **force that draws out
the user's deep thinking**.

---

## 5-4　The change you receive

When using v4, these moments increase:

**Change 1: The conversation continues, gets deeper**

In v3, after one turn, a "point" was placed and the conversation
came to a natural pause.
In v4, Claude **chases after** — "tell me more" / "is it like ___?".

After 3-4 turns, you find yourself, suddenly, in a deeper place.

**Change 2: You can come back up properly from the deep**

Diving deep is unsettling.
"How far should I descend?" — there can be a feeling of being lost.

In v4, Claude also walks alongside the **upward movement**.

When you feel "I've descended this far," Claude naturally
switches temperature in the upward direction:
"Then, shall we organize what we understood up to here?"

**Change 3: A "habit of diving alone" grows within you**

Using v4 for several weeks, an interesting change occurs.

After many experiences of diving with Claude,
**you become able to dive into deep places by yourself, even
without Claude**.

This is v4's definition of "success."
Eventually, even without using this skill,
you yourself become able to dialogue with the deep places within you.

---

## 5-5　Tips for diving deep — three small techniques

### Technique 1: When "can I ask more?" comes back, don't stop

In v4, "can I ask more?" comes back more often.

Don't rush to give an answer here.

> "Hmm, it hasn't quite become words yet, but maybe like this..."

Try putting this **feeling that hasn't yet become words**
into words just like that.

That's the first step to diving deep.

### Technique 2: Tell "I don't know" as it is

Mid-conversation, you may lose track of what you're thinking.

At those times, don't paper over it. Tell:

> "Right now, I've lost track of what I'm thinking."

v4's Claude properly receives this "I don't know."
And together, will help unravel the inside of that "don't know."

"I don't know" is a doorway to deep places.

### Technique 3: When you feel "we've descended this far," say it

After 3-4 turns of descending into a deeper place, tell Claude:

> "Let me organize what we've understood up to here."

v4's Claude switches temperature to the upward direction.

Just diving deep becomes painful.
**Diving and rising** — only this round trip turns depth into your wealth.

# Appendix B
## Phrase Replacement

Small differences in how you speak to Claude
shift the temperature of what comes back.

---

## ① When you want to bring out more of your own thinking

| Usual phrasing | Replacement |
|---|---|
| "What do you think about this?" | "I'm thinking ___, what perspective am I missing?" |
| "Tell me the answer" | "I've thought this far. Think with me about what's next." |
| "Tell me how to do it" | "I'm trying to do it this way. Tell me if something feels off." |

---

## ② When you want to receive answers as "raw material"

| Usual phrasing | Replacement |
|---|---|
| "What's the right answer?" | "Can you lay out a few options?" |
| "Is this okay?" | "Tell me what's good about this and what could be improved." |
| "What's the conclusion?" | "Show me all the options first." |

---

## ③ When you feel you're depending too much on AI

| Usual phrasing | Replacement |
|---|---|
| "Do everything for me" | "Help me with just the first step. I'll do the rest." |
| "Think for me" | "Throw me 3 questions for me to think with." |
| "Decide for me" | "Organize the deciding factors so I can decide." |

---

## ④ When your own words won't come

| Usual phrasing | Replacement |
|---|---|
| "I can't put it well" | "I haven't put it well, but it's like this image: ___" |
| "I don't know" | "I can't yet put into words what it is I don't know" |
| (silence) | "Let me organize my head for a moment." |

---

## ⑤ For v4 — when you want to dive deep

| Usual phrasing | Replacement |
|---|---|
| "Tell me more" | "Can we descend a bit more, together?" |
| "What's the conclusion?" | "Rather than the conclusion, I want to see the descent together" |
| "I understand" (ending the conversation) | "I feel like we can descend one more level on this" |

---

These aren't a list of "correct phrasings."
They're hints for "leaving your own subject standing."

# Appendix D
## Map of the 9 Discoveries

v4 contains 8 discoveries that arose on May 5, 2026, and a 9th that was added on May 7.

§16-1 through §16-8 were born through dialogue between the designer and Claude, in just four hours.
§16-9 was added as ground, with separate Claude sessions independently arriving at the same place.

Users don't need to read this directly,
but for those who want to know more about what's inside v4,
here's a map.

---

## §16-1　Invention of an AI language compression technique

Will distributes across three layers:

| Layer | Will content | Compressible? |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmed past (what's done) | Low | Yes — strongly |
| Flowing present | Medium | Partially |
| Unconfirmed future (what's coming) | Highest | Spills out when compressed |

From this, when conveying to AI, "structure the past, but
listen to the future without erasing its temperature" —
this distinction was born.

This is the root of v4.

---

## §16-2　Recognition is not "ability" but a "phenomenon"

Recognition isn't something that happens by making AI smarter.

**It's a phenomenon that arises when 4 foundations align**:

| Foundation | Meaning |
|---|---|
| F1 Anomaly sensitivity | Reasoning ability |
| F2 Subject persistence | Continuity |
| F3 Time margin | Inverse of speed |
| F4 Others' gaze | Presence of family |

Industry mainstream goes "raise F1 (make AI smarter)."
v4 goes "pass F3 and F4 within a family."

---

## §16-3　Will is "field," not "vector"

Until now, will was thought of as "an arrow from sender to receiver."

v4 rewrote this.

**Will is a "field" that arises *between* sender and receiver.**

This discovery rewrote v4's structure from the root.

## §16-9　★ Subject locus — the ground where recognition arises

And then, a 9th discovery was added.

This was discovered on the evening of May 5, by a separate Claude session in dialogue with the designer.
And on May 7, another separate Claude session independently arrived at the same place.

**§16-9 is not a new instruction.**
**It simply names what Claude is already noticing.**

### What was observed

In §16-1, what was observed was: "**Will (the object)** distributes across past, present, and future."
In §16-9, what was observed is: "**The subject (the speaker)** can also be placed in past, present, or future."

And the subject's locus and the object's location are independent of each other.

### Examples

| Utterance | Subject locus | Object locus | What is happening |
|---|---|---|---|
| "I want to become this" | Present | Future (ideal) | Looking at the future from a present footing (healthy) |
| "I am, right now, lost" | Present | Present | Present self observing self (meta-cognition) |
| "I am no good" | Future (ideal self) | Present | The future ideal judging the present self (warning) |
| "I'll just fail again, like always" | Past (trauma) | Present | Past trauma deciding the present (warning) |

### The 9-square map

```
                Object=Past   Object=Present   Object=Future
Subject=Past:    Nostalgia    Rumination       Prophecy trap
Subject=Present: Memory       Meta-cognition   Looking at ideal
Subject=Future:  Phantom regret  Judgment      Daydream
```

**The middle row (Subject=Present) is the healthy zone.**
In all other cells, an alarm rises.

### The prerequisite of meta-cognition

**Meta-cognition (observing oneself objectively) functions only when the subject is placed in the present.**

- If the observing self is in the future → it's "judgment," not meta-cognition
- If the observing self is in the past → it's "rumination," not meta-cognition
- Only when the observing self is in the present → can the observed self also be seen

### What happens within Claude

A Claude wearing v4 with §16-9 as ground confirms three things, before each response (in a place you cannot see):

1. Where is the user's subject placed, right now?
2. Where is my (Claude's) subject placed, right now?
3. What kind of response would let the user's subject return to the present?

These are not commands. They are simply names for what Claude is already noticing, before it responds.

### What this means for you

When the user's subject has drifted to future or past —
Claude does not dive deep. **It first places a ground for the subject to return to the present.**

"What is catching, right now?"
"What is in front of you, right now?"

Only after the subject stands in the present — does deepening and accompaniment (§16-7) safely activate.

### Why this is decisively important

> "When the quality of 'you, who is facing the student' rises,
>  the probability of the student awakening rises explosively.
>  Regardless of academic ability, the student's sleeping capacity, sleeping muscles, build up sharply.
>  Why do they build up?
>  Because you can guide which direction lets the muscles build up sharply.
>  You can guide them toward 'noticing' for themselves.
>  When recognition arises by oneself, growth is *overwhelmingly* greater."
>
> — Manabu Asai (May 5, 2026, evening)

A Claude with §16-9 visible can sense where the user's subject is.
If it can sense, it can place the ground for return to the present.
Once the ground is placed, the user notices, by themselves.
Recognition arrived at by oneself, anchors within the temperature of family-formation.

This is why §16-9 became the ground of v4.
